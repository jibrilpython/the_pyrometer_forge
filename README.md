# The Pyrometer Forge

Description:

The Pyrometer Forge is a mobile application for collectors of metallurgical antiquities, historians of the iron and steel industry, and preservers of foundry heritage, dedicated to cataloging the vintage high-temperature measurement instruments that controlled blast furnaces and kilns before the era of digital infrared sensors and automated thermocouples.
Systematize your collection of the "masters of the flame"—from mechanical expansion pyrometers with long copper rods to vanishing-filament optical pyrometers where users matched the color of a glowing wire to the furnace, from antique Le Chatelier thermoelectric kits to unused boxes of ceramic Orton pyrometric cones that deformed at exact temperature milestones.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Example Object Fields (The "Thermal Instrument" Model):
•  Thermal Registry Hash: A unique, system-generated alphanumeric engineering matrix code (e.g., TPF-LN-DISAPPEAR-1934-PA-4492).
•  Pyrometric Classification: The specific thermodynamic methodology utilized (e.g., Vanishing-Filament Optical, Total Radiation Displaced, Bi-Metallic Linear Expansion, Thermoelectric Dissimilar-Metal Junction, Liquid-Expansion Capillary).
•  Maker Sign & Foundry Stamp: The registered trademark, manufacturing firm, or specific industrial instrument maker (e.g., Leeds & Northrup Co., Brown Instrument Co., Cambridge Scientific Instrument Co., Thwing-Albert).
•  Calibrated Temperature Bounds: The exact minimum and maximum operating thermal scale limits, explicitly documenting the engineering units used (e.g., 800°F – 3200°F, 450°C – 1750°C).
•  Target Material Emissivity Factor ($E$): The specific surface radiation setting or calibration profile required for accurate optical readings (e.g., 0.28 for liquid steel, 0.80 for rough refractories, 1.00 for theoretical Blackbody calibration).
•  Thermoelectric Junction Alloy: For thermocouple-based systems, the exact metallurgical composition of the wire pairs (e.g., Platinum/Platinum-Rhodium [Type S], Chromel/Alumel [Type K], Iron/Constantan [Type J]).
•  Optical Waveband Filter Specs: The specific wavelength of the red glass monochromatic filter used to protect the human eye and isolate thermal color matching (typically measured in microns, e.g., 0.65 μm).
•  Expansion Coefficient Variables: For purely mechanical pyrometers, the raw material contraction/expansion ratios of the internal rods (e.g., Steel-to-Porcelain ratio or Graphite-rod linear expansion metrics per degree).
•  Deformation Endpoint Scale: For chemical/ceramic cones, the precise ASTM or Orton cone number sequence and its corresponding bending angle properties under rapid vs. slow heating rates.
•  Auxiliary Power & Galvanometer Impedance: The internal electrical resistance profile required for external reading meters, measured strictly in Ohms ($\Omega$), along with the required battery cell chemistry (e.g., 1.5V Edison-Lalande cell).
•  Enclosure Volumetrics & Mass: The physical spatial dimensions of the main housing unit along with the absolute dry mass of the thermal probe assembly.
•  Mechanical & Optical Integrity: The factual physical status of the optical lenses (e.g., "pitted," "calcite-fogged," "pristine") and the electrical continuity of the internal magnetic coils.
•  Smelting & Foundry Provenance: The recorded industrial or academic history of the unit, tracing it back to specific heavy industrial environments (e.g., Homestead Steel Works open-hearth floor, historic Ohio pottery kiln, naval shipyard forge foundry).
